Today's technology, tomorrow's problem.


But we can stop it.

When the technology is thrown into a landfill, dangerous chemicals can seep into the environment and cause damage throughout the food chain as plants and animals consume contaminated water and through biomagnification. That's why it's illegal in New York State.

Luckily, correct, environmentally-friendly, and legal disposal of e-waste is extremely easy in Ithaca, New York. All you have to do is identify what type of e-waste you have, figure out what should happen to it, and bring it to the appropriate place.

Learn about the specific dangers and specifically how you should dispose of old electronics.




The Tompkins County Solid Waste Management Division describes its mission as the management of solid waste in an “environmentally sound, cost-effective, socially responsible and safe” way. This environmentally conscious organization has its recycling center located at 160 Commercial Avenue, and accepts yard waste, plastics, tires, clothing, and a variety of e-waste. The Recycling and Solid Waste Center (RSWC) also accepts hazardous wastes at six dates throughout the year. Dropping your e-waste off at the RSWC ensures that it will be disposed of responsibly and safely.
